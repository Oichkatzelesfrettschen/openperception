Discussion

5

Recent developments in high-field MRI have enabled the study of small brain structures such

6

as the subdivisions of human thalamic nuclei in-vivo. We here used this technical advance to

7

image the human LGN and its M and P subdivisions in a large sample of adults with DD and

8

matched control participants. Consistent with human post-mortem reports dating back to the

9

1990s,4,5 we found that individuals with DD show functional and structural response alterations

10

specifically in the M-LGN. Our findings solve the long-standing question of whether M-LGN

11

alterations are also present in DD in-vivo and give first indications about their potential sex-

12

specific behavioral relevance.

Our finding of different lateralization of the M-LGN in DD compared to controls is compatible

15

with previous findings of left-lateralized sensory thalamic alterations in human DD. 5,9,19 In the

16

auditory pathway, histological changes occurred specifically in the left MGB in post-mortem

17

brains of dyslexics.5 In-vivo MRI studies on DD showed functional response changes and

18

altered connectivity of the MGB, which were restricted to the left hemisphere. 9,19 Previous

19

findings on potential laterality of thalamic alterations in the visual processing pathway are less

20

conclusive4,10 : histopathological changes were found in the M but not P layers of the LGN;

21

however, it is unclear which hemisphere(s) were affected. 4 In addition, there is reduced

22

23

13
