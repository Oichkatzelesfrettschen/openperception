## 1. Enhanced Perceptual Functioning Theory

### Foundational Papers

**Mottron, L., Dawson, M., Soulieres, I., Hubert, B., & Burack, J. (2006)**
- **Title:** Enhanced Perceptual Functioning in Autism: An Update, and Eight Principles of Autistic Perception
- **Journal:** Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders, 36(1), 27-43
- **DOI:** 10.1007/s10803-005-0040-7
- **URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10803-005-0040-7
- **PDF:** https://www.researchgate.net/publication/7321285_Enhanced_Perceptual_Functioning_in_Autism_An_Update_and_Eight_Principles_of_Autistic_Perception
- **Description:** Proposes the EPF model encompassing eight principles of autistic perception: locally oriented visual and auditory perception, enhanced low-level discrimination, use of posterior networks in complex visual tasks, enhanced perception of static stimuli, diminished perception of complex movement, autonomy of low-level processing, and differential relation between perception and intelligence.

**Mottron, L., & Burack, J. A. (2001)**
- **Title:** Enhanced perceptual functioning in the development of autism
- **Book Chapter:** In Burack, Charman, Yirmiya, & Zelazo (Eds.), The development of autism: Perspectives from theory and research (pp. 131-148)
- **Publisher:** Lawrence Erlbaum Associates
- **Description:** Early formulation of enhanced perceptual functioning in autism development.

**Samson, F., Mottron, L., Soulieres, I., & Zeffiro, T. A. (2011)**
- **Title:** Enhanced visual functioning in autism: an ALE meta-analysis
- **Journal:** Human Brain Mapping, 33(7), 1553-1581
- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6870295/
- **Description:** Meta-analysis demonstrating enhanced visual functioning and stronger engagement of perceptual brain regions in autism.

**Bonnel, A., Mottron, L., Peretz, I., Trudel, M., Gallun, E., & Bonnel, A. M. (2003)**
- **Title:** Enhanced pitch sensitivity in individuals with autism: a signal detection analysis
- **Journal:** Journal of Cognitive Neuroscience, 15(2), 226-235
- **Description:** Demonstrates enhanced auditory perception in autism supporting EPF theory.

### Recent EPF Research

**Heaton, P., & Wallace, G. L. (2004)**
- **Title:** Annotation: The savant syndrome
- **Journal:** Journal of Child Psychology and Psychiatry, 45(5), 899-911
- **Description:** Reviews savant syndrome and its relationship to enhanced perceptual functioning in autism.

## 2. Weak Central Coherence

### Foundational Papers

**Happe, F., & Frith, U. (2006)**
- **Title:** The weak coherence account: detail-focused cognitive style in autism spectrum disorders
- **Journal:** Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders, 36(1), 5-25
- **Description:** Comprehensive review of weak central coherence theory, proposing a cognitive style rather than deficit.

### Recent Research (2023-2024)

**Authors: Various (2024)**
- **Title:** Weak central coherence in neurodevelopmental disorders: a comparative study
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Psychology
- **URL:** https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1348074/full
- **Description:** Comparative study examining weak central coherence across neurodevelopmental disorders including ASD, ADHD, and NVLD in 252 children aged 6-11.

**Lopez, B., et al. (2022)**
- **Title:** Weak central coherence in adults with ASD: Evidence from eye-tracking and thematic content analysis of social scenes
- **Journal:** PLOS ONE
- **PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35450487/
- **Description:** Eye-tracking study demonstrating local processing tendency in adults with ASD and its impact on social scene understanding.

**Manning, C. (2024)**
- **Title:** Visual processing and decision-making in autism and dyslexia: Insights from cross-syndrome approaches
- **Journal:** Quarterly Journal of Experimental Psychology
- **URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/17470218241264627
- **PDF:** https://pure-oai.bham.ac.uk/ws/portalfiles/portal/235159224/manning-2024-visual-processing-and-decision-making-in-autism-and-dyslexia-insights-from-cross-syndrome-approaches.pdf
- **Description:** Cross-syndrome comparison of visual processing in autism and dyslexia, examining global motion processing and local-global balance.

## 3. Sensory Processing Differences

## 6. Motion Perception

### Meta-Analyses

**Van der Hallen, R., et al. (2019)**
- **Title:** Global Motion Perception in Autism Spectrum Disorder: A Meta-Analysis
- **Journal:** Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders
- **URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10803-019-04194-8
- **Description:** Meta-analysis of 48 articles demonstrating small global motion processing deficit in ASD for both biological and coherent motion.

### Recent Research

**Manning, C. (2024)**
- **Title:** Visual processing and decision-making in autism and dyslexia
- **Journal:** Quarterly Journal of Experimental Psychology
- **URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/17470218241264627
- **Description:** Examines motion coherence processing differences in autism.

**Cusack, J. P., et al. (2024)**
- **Title:** Top-down biological motion perception does not differ between adults scoring high versus low on autism traits
- **Journal:** Biological Psychology
- **URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0301051124000796
- **Description:** Study finding no evidence for diminished biological motion perception but different visual processing style focusing on single percepts.

## 7. Predictive Coding and Bayesian Frameworks

### Theoretical Framework Papers

**Pellicano, E., & Burr, D. (2012)**
- **Title:** When the world becomes "too real": a Bayesian explanation of autistic perception
- **Journal:** Trends in Cognitive Sciences, 16(10), 504-510

**Van de Cruys, S., et al. (2014)**
- **Title:** Precise minds in uncertain worlds: predictive coding in autism

## 9. Local-Global Processing

### Meta-Analyses

**Van der Hallen, R., Evers, K., Brewaeys, K., Van den Noortgate, W., & Wagemans, J. (2015)**
- **Title:** Global processing takes time: A meta-analysis on local-global visual processing in ASD
- **Journal:** Psychological Bulletin, 141(3), 549-573
- **PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25420221/
- **Description:** Meta-analysis of 56 articles finding slow global processing rather than local enhancement or global deficit in ASD.

**Various Authors (2025)**
- **Title:** Local and Global Visual Processing in Autism: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Neuroimaging Studies
- **Journal:** Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders
- **URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10803-025-07061-x
- **Description:** ALE meta-analysis of 15 neuroimaging studies examining local and global visual processing brain mechanisms.

### Empirical Studies

**Petrinovic, M., et al. (2020)**
- **Title:** Global and local visual processing in autism: a co-twin-control study
- **Journal:** Journal of Child Psychology and Psychiatry
- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7155117/
- **PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31452200/
- **Description:** Twin study finding association between autism and reduced global visual processing.
