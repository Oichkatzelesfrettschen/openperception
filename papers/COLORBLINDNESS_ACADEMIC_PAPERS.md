## Recent Papers (2023-2025)

### Comprehensive Reviews

**1. Dyschromatopsia: A Comprehensive Analysis of Mechanisms and Cutting-Edge Treatments for Color Vision Deficiency**
- **Authors:** Zihao Yang, Lin Yan, Wenliang Zhang, Jia Qi, Wenjing An, Kai Yao
- **Year:** 2024 (Published January 17, 2024)
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Neuroscience, Volume 18
- **DOI:** 10.3389/fnins.2024.1265630
- **URL:** https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnins.2024.1265630/full
- **Access:** Open Access (CC BY)
- **Description:** Comprehensive review of mechanisms supporting human color vision and alterations in dyschromatopsia. Covers gene therapy, pharmacological interventions, and visual aids for treatment.

**2. An Integrative Review for Clinical Evaluation of Color Vision: The Right Test for the Right Disease**

- **PMC:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12487792/
- **Access:** Open Access (CC BY-NC-SA 4.0)
- **Description:** Integrative review of different color vision tests, their construction specificities, and applications in ophthalmological and neurological diseases.

**3. A Global Perspective of Color Vision Deficiency: Awareness, Diagnosis, and Lived Experiences**
- **Year:** 2024
- **PMC:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12385717/
- **Access:** Open Access
- **Description:** Global overview of CVD covering awareness, diagnostic approaches, and patient experiences.

**4. Prevalence of Color Vision Deficiency in Africa: Systematic Review and Meta-analysis**
- **Year:** 2024 (Published December 4, 2024)
- **Journal:** PLoS ONE
- **PMC:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11616826/
- **PROSPERO Registration:** CRD42024510403
- **Access:** Open Access (CC BY)
- **Description:** Systematic review determining the prevalence of CVD in Africa through comprehensive literature search of PubMed/MEDLINE/EMBASE.

## Foundational/Seminal Papers

### John Dalton's Original Work

**11. Extraordinary Facts Relating to the Vision of Colours**
- **Author:** John Dalton
- **Year:** 1798
- **Description:** First scientific description of color blindness by the English chemist who was himself colorblind. Foundation of the scientific study of color vision deficiencies. The condition is still sometimes called "Daltonism" in his honor.

### Opsin Gene Discovery

**12. Molecular Genetics of Human Color Vision: The Genes Encoding Blue, Green, and Red Pigments**
- **Authors:** Jeremy Nathans, Darcy Thomas, David S. Hogness
- **Year:** 1986
- **Journal:** Science
- **DOI:** 10.1126/science.2937147
- **PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2937147/
- **URL:** https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.2937147
- **Description:** Landmark paper describing isolation and sequencing of genomic and cDNA clones encoding the three human color pigment apoproteins. Found red and green pigments show 96% mutual identity but only 43% identity with blue pigment.

**13. The Genetics of Normal and Defective Color Vision**
- **Authors:** Jay Neitz, Maureen Neitz
- **Year:** 2011
- **Journal:** Vision Research
- **PMC:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3075382/
- **Access:** Open Access (PMC)
- **Description:** Comprehensive review of color vision genetics. Revealed high frequency of recombination between L and M opsin genes is responsible for many peculiarities of red-green color vision.

**14. Tandem Array of Human Visual Pigment Genes at Xq28**
- **Year:** 1989
- **Journal:** Science
- **DOI:** 10.1126/science.2837827
- **URL:** https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.2837827
- **Description:** Described the tandem array organization of L and M opsin genes on the X chromosome.

### Ishihara Test Development

**15. Tests for Colour-Blindness (Ishihara Original)**
- **Author:** Shinobu Ishihara
- **Year:** 1917 (first published)
- **Description:** Original publication of the Ishihara pseudo-isochromatic plate test for detection of red-green color deficiencies. Developed by Professor Ishihara at the University of Tokyo. Remains the most widely used screening test for color vision deficiency worldwide.

## Clinical Diagnostic Studies

**16. Can Protanopia Be Correctly Diagnosed in Clinical Practice? An Evaluation of Diagnosis by Four Screening Tests**
- **Year:** 2020
- **PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33055513/
- **Description:** Found that the Ishihara test misdiagnosed all 18 protanopes as having a deutan deficiency. Hardy-Rand-Rittler and Mollon-Reffin tests correctly identified protan CVD in 100% of protanopes.

## Simulation Algorithms and Accessibility

### Foundational Simulation Algorithms

**32. Computerized Simulation of Color Appearance for Dichromats**
- **Authors:** Hans Brettel, Francoise Vienot, John D. Mollon
- **Year:** 1997
- **Journal:** Journal of the Optical Society of America A
- **DOI:** 10.1364/JOSAA.14.002647
- **URL:** https://opg.optica.org/josaa/abstract.cfm?uri=josaa-14-10-2647
- **PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9316278/
- **Description:** Foundational algorithm for simulating dichromatic color vision. Transforms color stimuli as projections onto a reduced stimulus surface defined by neutral axis and specific monochromatic stimuli (575nm yellow and 475nm blue for protan/deutan; 660nm red and 485nm blue-green for tritan).

**33. Machado-Oliveira-Fernandes Model for Anomalous Trichromacy Simulation**
- **Authors:** Gustavo M. Machado, Manuel M. Oliveira, Leandro A. F. Fernandes
- **Year:** 2009
- **Description:** Physiologically-based simulation for deuteranomaly, protanomaly, and tritanomaly. Provides unified approach covering both dichromacy and anomalous trichromacy with severity parameter (0=normal, 1=dichromacy).

## Statistics Summary

- **Prevalence:** ~8% of males and 0.5% of females have red-green CVD
- **Most common type:** Deuteranomaly (difficulty distinguishing green shades)
- **Tritanopia prevalence:** ~1 in 10,000 (much rarer than red-green)
- **Genetic basis:** X-linked for red-green; Chromosome 7 (autosomal) for blue-yellow
- **Global affected population:** ~350 million people worldwide with some form of CVD
