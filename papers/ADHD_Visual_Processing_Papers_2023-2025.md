# ADHD Visual and Perceptual Processing: Academic Papers (2023-2025)

A comprehensive compendium of recent academic research on visual processing, attention, and perceptual mechanisms in ADHD.

---

## 1. VISUAL PROCESSING AND PERCEPTUAL OSCILLATIONS

### 1.1 Visual Processing Oscillates Differently Through Time for Adults with ADHD
- **Authors:** Pelland-Goulet P, Arguin M, Brisebois H, Gosselin N
- **Year:** 2025
- **Journal:** PLOS ONE
- **DOI:** [10.1371/journal.pone.0310605](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0310605)
- **URL:** https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0310605
- **Description:** Compared temporal features of visual processing between ADHD and neurotypical individuals using a temporal sampling technique. Machine learning classified participants with 91.8% accuracy based on perceptual oscillation patterns. Secondary findings showed 91.3% classification accuracy regarding psychostimulant medication usage.

### 1.2 Neural Responses to Global and Local Visual Information Processing Provide Neural Signatures of ADHD Symptoms
- **Authors:** Multiple authors (International study, N=465)
- **Year:** 2025
- **Journal:** International Journal of Psychophysiology
- **DOI:** [10.1016/j.ijpsycho.2025.112500](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0167876025000789)
- **Description:** Used EEG recordings with ensemble perception tasks. Found inattention symptoms associated with early responses to global stimuli, suggesting reduced global prioritization. Challenges earlier theories by showing ADHD includes deficits in both global and local processing.

### 1.3 Gestalt, Navon and Kanizsa Illusion Processing in CVI, ADHD, and Dyslexia Children
- **Authors:** Hokken MJ, Van Der Zee YJ, Pereira RR, et al.
- **Year:** 2024
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Human Neuroscience
- **DOI:** [10.3389/fnhum.2024.1496796](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnhum.2024.1496796)
- **URL:** https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/human-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnhum.2024.1496796/full
- **Description:** Examined Global Visual Selective Attention (VSA) in children with various neurodevelopmental disorders. Found similar VSA deficits across CVI, ADHD, and dyslexia populations.

### 1.4 Perception of Visual Illusions in Children and Teenagers With ADHD
- **Authors:** Montiel V, Navarrete V, Gonzalez-Perez AM, et al.
- **Year:** 2025/2026
- **Journal:** Journal of Attention Disorders
- **DOI:** [10.1177/10870547251369665](https://doi.org/10.1177/10870547251369665)
- **URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/10870547251369665
- **Description:** Compared illusory perception between children with ADHD and neurotypical peers across geometric, contrast, movement, and contour illusions. Found specific deficit in top-down visual completion processes (Kanizsa triangle).

## 2. EYE TRACKING AND VISUAL ATTENTION

### 2.1 Development of an Innovative Approach Using Portable Eye Tracking to Assist ADHD Screening
- **Authors:** Yoo JH, Kang C, Lim JS, et al.
- **Year:** 2024
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Psychiatry
- **DOI:** [10.3389/fpsyt.2024.1337595](https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyt.2024.1337595)
- **URL:** https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychiatry/articles/10.3389/fpsyt.2024.1337595/full
- **PMC:** PMC10902460
- **Description:** Used machine learning with portable eye tracking data from 56 ADHD children and 79 controls. Achieved 76.3% classification accuracy. Identified 33 eye-tracking features distinguishing ADHD from typical development.

### 2.2 Virtual Reality-Assisted Prediction of Adult ADHD Based on Eye Tracking, EEG, Actigraphy and Behavioral Indices
- **Authors:** Wiebe A, Selaskowski B, Paskin S, et al.
- **Year:** 2024
- **Journal:** Translational Psychiatry
- **DOI:** [10.1038/s41398-024-03217-y](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41398-024-03217-y)
- **URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41398-024-03217-y
- **Description:** Investigated multimodal data for ADHD prediction including eye tracking, EEG, and actigraphy using machine learning analysis.

### 2.3 The Use of Eye-Tracking Technology in Supporting Children and Adolescents with ADHD: A Comprehensive Review
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2025
- **Journal:** Disability and Rehabilitation: Assistive Technology
- **DOI:** [10.1080/17483107.2025.2497305](https://doi.org/10.1080/17483107.2025.2497305)
- **URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/17483107.2025.2497305
- **Description:** Comprehensive review of eye-tracking applications in ADHD. Found key metrics (saccades, fixations, blink rates, pupil dilation) consistently linked to attentional control deficits.

### 2.4 Use of Eye Tracking to Improve the Identification of ADHD in Children
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2023
- **Journal:** Scientific Reports
- **DOI:** [10.1038/s41598-023-41654-9](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-023-41654-9)
- **URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-023-41654-9
- **Description:** Eye-tracking alone showed AUC of 0.856 for ADHD identification, outperforming CPTs alone (0.769). Eye movement scales significantly improved after ADHD medication.

### 2.5 Eye-Tracking Training Improves Visuospatial Working Memory of Children with ADHD and ASD
- **Authors:** Chan AS, et al.
- **Year:** 2024
- **Journal:** Autism Research
- **DOI:** [10.1002/aur.3238](https://doi.org/10.1002/aur.3238)
- **URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/aur.3238
- **Description:** Demonstrated that eye-tracking-based training can improve cognitive performance in both ADHD and ASD populations.

### 2.6 Eyes on CVI: Eye Movements Unveil Distinct Visual Search Patterns in Cerebral Visual Impairment Compared to ADHD, Dyslexia, and Neurotypical Children
- **Authors:** Hokken MJ, et al.
- **Year:** 2024
- **Journal:** Research in Developmental Disabilities
- **DOI:** [10.1016/j.ridd.2024.104767](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ridd.2024.104767)
- **Description:** Developed novel test battery coupling visual search tasks with verbal and gaze-based measures to differentiate neurodevelopmental disorders.

## 3. SELECTIVE VISUAL ATTENTION

### 3.1 Selective Visual Attention in ADHD: A Narrative Review
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2025
- **Journal:** Current Neurology and Neuroscience Reports
- **DOI:** [10.1007/s11910-025-01435-5](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11910-025-01435-5)
- **URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11910-025-01435-5
- **PMC:** PMC12289795
- **Description:** Comprehensive review characterizing selective visual attention in ADHD. Found both bottom-up (stimulus-driven) and top-down (goal-driven) attention affected. Identified potential issues at the perceptual processing level.

### 3.2 Visual Selective Attention and Visual Search Performance in Children with CVI, ADHD, and Dyslexia: A Scoping Review
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2022 (cited in 2024 research)
- **Journal:** Child Neuropsychology
- **DOI:** [10.1080/09297049.2022.2057940](https://doi.org/10.1080/09297049.2022.2057940)
- **URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/09297049.2022.2057940
- **Description:** Scoping review on visual selective attention dysfunctions. Found visual search performance impairments in ADHD characterized by poor accuracy rather than reaction time, particularly in inattentive and combined types.

## 4. VISUAL WORKING MEMORY AND NEUROIMAGING

### 4.1 Working Memory Related Functional Connectivity in Adult ADHD and Its Amenability to Training
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2024
- **Journal:** NeuroImage: Clinical
- **DOI:** [10.1016/j.nicl.2024.103581](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2213158224001372)
- **Description:** fMRI study of 42 adults with ADHD and 36 controls during visuospatial and verbal n-back tasks. Found decreased functional connectivity in fronto-parietal, temporal, occipital, cerebellar, and subcortical regions.

### 4.2 Advances in the Etiology and Neuroimaging of Children with ADHD
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2024
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Pediatrics
- **DOI:** [10.3389/fped.2024.1400468](https://doi.org/10.3389/fped.2024.1400468)
- **URL:** https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/pediatrics/articles/10.3389/fped.2024.1400468/full
- **Description:** Review highlighting that working memory brain regions show weaker activation in ADHD. Found left DLPFC more active during working memory tasks using fNIRS.

### 4.3 Functional Neuroimaging of Visuospatial Working Memory Tasks Enables Accurate Detection of ADHD
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2015 (foundational study cited in 2024 reviews)
- **Journal:** NeuroImage: Clinical
- **DOI:** [10.1016/j.nicl.2015.11.014](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2213158215001539)
- **Description:** Demonstrated 92.5% classification accuracy for ADHD detection using fMRI data from four visuospatial working memory tasks.

## 5. RETINAL AND DOPAMINERGIC VISUAL FUNCTION

### 5.1 Retinal Fundus Imaging as Biomarker for ADHD Using Machine Learning for Screening and Visual Attention Stratification
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2025
- **Journal:** npj Digital Medicine
- **DOI:** [10.1038/s41746-025-01547-9](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41746-025-01547-9)
- **URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41746-025-01547-9
- **PMC:** PMC11914053
- **Description:** Used machine learning on retinal fundus photographs from 323 children/adolescents. ADHD screening achieved 95.5%-96.9% AUROC. Visual attention stratification showed strong performance (AUROC >85%).

### 5.2 Colour Vision in ADHD: Part 1 - Testing the Retinal Dopaminergic Hypothesis
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2014 (foundational, cited in current research)
- **Journal:** Behavioral and Brain Functions
- **DOI:** [10.1186/1744-9081-10-38](https://doi.org/10.1186/1744-9081-10-38)
- **PMC:** PMC4219036
- **Description:** Found females with ADHD less accurate in discriminating blue and red color saturation. Color perception difficulties restricted to blue spectrum, supporting retinal dopaminergic hypothesis.

### 5.3 Color Vision in ADHD: Part 2 - Does Attention Influence Color Perception?
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2014
- **Journal:** Behavioral and Brain Functions
- **DOI:** [10.1186/1744-9081-10-39](https://doi.org/10.1186/1744-9081-10-39)
- **PMC:** PMC4282194
- **Description:** Found exogenous covert attention intact in ADHD adults; attention does not account for chromatic saturation perception impairments.

### 5.4 Color Vision in ADHD: A Pilot Visual Evoked Potential Study
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2014
- **Journal:** Spanish Journal of Psychology
- **DOI:** [10.1016/S1888-9891(14)00097-1](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1888429614000971)
- **PMC:** PMC4401829
- **Description:** Found larger P1 amplitude for blue-yellow stimuli in ADHD, possibly reflecting compensatory strategy for compromised retinal input.

### 5.5 Normalization of Increased Retinal Background Noise After ADHD Treatment
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2019 (cited in current research)
- **Journal:** Biological Psychiatry
- **DOI:** [10.1016/j.biopsych.2019.04.005](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31053491/)
- **Description:** Found elevated retinal background noise in ADHD (0.079 microV vs 0.062 microV in controls), which normalized after methylphenidate treatment.

## 6. MAGNOCELLULAR PATHWAY AND MOTION PROCESSING

### 6.1 Efficiency in Magnocellular Processing: A Common Deficit in Neurodevelopmental Disorders
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2020 (cited in 2024 research)
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Human Neuroscience
- **DOI:** [10.3389/fnhum.2020.00049](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnhum.2020.00049)
- **PMC:** PMC7057243
- **URL:** https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fnhum.2020.00049/full
- **Description:** Found ASD and dyslexia showed lower flicker fusion thresholds compared to controls, but singular ADHD diagnosis did not differ from controls. Suggests magnocellular pathway inefficiency is not primary to ADHD alone.

### 6.2 Atypical Biological Motion Perception in Children with ADHD: Local Motion and Global Configuration Processing
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2024
- **Journal:** eLife (Reviewed Preprint)
- **DOI:** [10.7554/eLife.90313](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.90313)
- **URL:** https://elifesciences.org/reviewed-preprints/90313v2
- **Description:** Identified atypical local and global biological motion perception in ADHD children. Local BM processing linked to social interaction traits; global BM processing showed age-related development patterns.

### 6.3 Cognitive Control Over Visual Motion Processing: Are Children With ADHD Especially Compromised?
- **Authors:** Multiple authors
- **Year:** 2018 (cited in 2024 reviews)
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Human Neuroscience
- **DOI:** [10.3389/fnhum.2018.00491](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnhum.2018.00491)
- **PMC:** PMC6290085
- **Description:** Found ADHD group showed less accuracy for moving stimuli and prolonged response times. Supports that cognitive control deficit in ADHD is higher-order and independent of stimulus type.

## SUMMARY OF KEY FINDINGS

### Visual Processing Characteristics in ADHD:

1. **Temporal Processing:** Distinct perceptual oscillation patterns detectable by machine learning (91.8% accuracy)

2. **Global-Local Processing:** Reduced global bias; deficits in both global and local processing (not limited to global alone)

3. **Eye Movement Abnormalities:** Increased saccade latency, decreased fixation time, excessive microsaccades and blinks

4. **Color Vision:** Blue spectrum perception deficits linked to retinal dopamine dysfunction; more pronounced in females

5. **Working Memory:** Decreased functional connectivity in fronto-parietal networks during visuospatial tasks

6. **Retinal Biomarkers:** Retinal fundus imaging achieves 95.5-96.9% AUROC for ADHD screening

7. **Motion Processing:** Atypical biological motion perception with distinct local vs. global processing patterns

8. **Peripheral Vision:** Self-reported difficulties; heightened sensitivity to peripheral stimuli; potential therapeutic target

9. **Magnocellular Pathway:** Not primarily affected in isolated ADHD (unlike dyslexia and ASD)

10. **Interface Design Implications:** Need for minimalist design, progressive disclosure, reduced visual clutter, and user-controlled focus modes

## 2025-2026 Additions

### Retinal Fundus Imaging as ADHD Biomarker (2025)

**Title**: "Retinal fundus imaging as biomarker for ADHD using machine learning
for screening and visual attention stratification"
**Journal**: npj Digital Medicine (Nature, 2025)
**DOI**: 10.1038/s41746-025-01547-9
**Key Finding**: Machine learning analysis of retinal fundus photographs for
noninvasive ADHD screening and executive function stratification:
- ADHD screening models: 95.5%-96.9% AUROC
- Visual subdomain models show strong performance for executive function stratification
- Noninvasive, objective, and scalable approach
**Accessibility Relevance**: Confirms visual pathway differences in ADHD that
may inform interface design (temporal processing, attention allocation)

### AI-Enabled Early ASD/ADHD Detection (2025)

**Title**: "AI-Enabled Technologies and Biomarker Analysis for the Early
Identification of Autism and Related Neurodevelopmental Disorders"
**Journal**: PMC (2025)
**Key Finding**: Review of AI-enabled tools combining machine learning with
biological and behavioral signatures. Eye-tracking demonstrates high diagnostic
accuracy often rivaling traditional observational methods.

### Eye Tracking and Gaze Patterns (2025)

Children with both autism and ADHD show shorter periods of focused attention on
faces while viewing static social cues compared to ASD-only and neurotypical
children. ADHD-specific gaze patterns are linked to task accuracy, with increased
fixation variability and shorter dwell times on task-relevant stimuli.

**Accessibility Implications**:
- UI elements must compete effectively for attention (contrast, size, placement)
- Critical information should not rely on sustained visual attention
- Progressive disclosure reduces visual competition
