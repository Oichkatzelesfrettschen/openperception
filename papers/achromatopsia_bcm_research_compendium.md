## Overview of Conditions

### Achromatopsia (Complete Color Blindness / Rod Monochromacy)
- **Prevalence**: 1 in 30,000 people worldwide
- **Inheritance**: Autosomal recessive
- **Causative Genes**: CNGA3, CNGB3, GNAT2, PDE6C, PDE6H, ATF6
  - CNGA3 and CNGB3 mutations account for 80-90% of cases
- **Clinical Features**: Complete absence of color discrimination, low visual acuity (~20/200), photophobia, nystagmus

### Blue Cone Monochromacy (BCM)
- **Prevalence**: Approximately 1 in 100,000 people worldwide
- **Inheritance**: X-linked recessive
- **Causative Genes**: OPN1LW/OPN1MW gene cluster on X-chromosome
- **Clinical Features**: Severely reduced L/M cone function, poor visual acuity, impaired color discrimination, myopia, nystagmus

## Achromatopsia Gene Therapy Trials

### CNGA3 Gene Therapy

#### German Trial (AAV8.CNGA3) - NCT02610582

**Title**: Three-year results of phase I retinal gene therapy trial for CNGA3-mutated achromatopsia: results of a non-randomised controlled trial

**Authors**: Fischer MD, Michalakis S, Wilhelm B, et al.

**Published**: British Journal of Ophthalmology, 2021

**DOI**: 10.1136/bjophthalmol-2020-318866

**PMID**: 34006508

**URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34006508/

**Description**: Open-label, nonrandomized controlled trial treating nine patients in three escalating dose groups with subretinal AAV8.CNGA3 gene therapy (November 2015 - October 2016). Three-year follow-up confirmed safety with no adverse or serious adverse events related to the study drug after year 1. Functional benefits noted at year 1 persisted through years 2 and 3.

---

#### AGTC Trial (AGTC-402) - NCT03758404

**Title**: Phase 1/2 Gene Therapy Clinical Trial for Achromatopsia (CNGA3 Mutations)

**Sponsor**: Applied Genetic Technologies Corporation (AGTC)

**Status**: Development discontinued

**Description**: AGTC did not observe consistent evidence of biologic activity in the Phase 1/2 clinical trial for AGTC-402. The company believes the difference in efficacy between CNGB3 and CNGA3 gene therapies may be due to differences in how mutated genes express defective proteins. Patients with CNGA3 mutations have defective proteins that may interfere with the function of healthy protein expressed by gene therapy.

### CNGB3 Gene Therapy

#### MeiraGTx/Janssen Trial (AAV8-hCARp.hCNGB3) - NCT03001310

**Title**: First-in-Human Gene Therapy Trial of AAV8-hCARp.hCNGB3 in Adults and Children With CNGB3-associated Achromatopsia

**Authors**: Michaelides M, Hirji N, Wong SC, et al.

**Published**: American Journal of Ophthalmology, 2023 Jul; 253:243-251

**DOI**: 10.1016/j.ajo.2023.05.011

**PMID**: 37172884

**URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37172884/

**Description**: Prospective, phase 1/2, open-label, nonrandomized clinical trial assessing safety and efficacy of AAV8-hCARp.hCNGB3 in 23 adults and children with CNGB3-associated achromatopsia. Treatment demonstrated acceptable safety and tolerability profile with improvements in several efficacy parameters.

---

#### AGTC Trial (AGTC-401) - NCT02935517

**Title**: Phase 1/2 Gene Therapy Clinical Trial for Achromatopsia (CNGB3 Mutations)

**Sponsor**: Applied Genetic Technologies Corporation

**Status**: Further development planned pending FDA discussions

**Description**: 31 patients treated (21 adults, 10 pediatric). Some patients at second-highest dose (1.1E+12 vg/mL) showed improvements in retinal sensitivity by static perimetry and light discomfort. AGTC plans to continue development of AGTC-401.

**URL**: https://www.fightingblindness.org/news/agtc-plans-further-clinical-development-of-achromatopsia-cngb3-gene-therapy-826

---

### Safety Concerns in High-Dose Trials

In both CNGA3 and CNGB3 trials, treatment with highest doses (3.2E+12 vg/mL) led to three cases of severe ocular inflammation in pediatric patients. All cases resolved or continue to resolve, with best corrected visual acuity returning to baseline.

## Rod Monochromacy Research

### Overview

Rod monochromacy (complete achromatopsia) is characterized by:
- Reduced visual acuity (~0.1 or 20/200)
- Total color blindness
- Photo-aversion
- Nystagmus
- Rods absent from fovea causing ~1.5 degree foveal scotoma
- Rod-mediated vision approximately 5x slower than cone-mediated vision

### Key 2024 Research

**Title**: Intact high-level visual functions in congenital rod-monochromacy

**Authors**: Shabat G, McKyton A, Elul G, et al.

**Journal**: Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2024 Oct; 18:1418916

**DOI**: 10.3389/fnins.2024.1418916

**URL**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnins.2024.1418916/full

**Key Findings**:
- Despite low-level visual limitations, ACHM patients demonstrate notable high-level visual skills
- Rod-only vision is sufficient for development of proficient reading and face recognition
- Reading speed, face recognition abilities, and susceptibility to face inversion not inferior to controls
- ACHM patients exhibited similar eye movements to controls, focusing attention on specific areas of words and faces

## ATF6 Achromatopsia Research

### Background

ATF6 is the only known achromatopsia gene that does not encode a phototransduction cascade protein. ATF6 is localized in the endoplasmic reticulum and is involved in ER stress and the unfolded protein response.

### Clinical Trial

**Title**: Investigation of Glycerol Phenylbutyrate (PBA) for ATF6 Achromatopsia

**Registry**: NCT04041232

**Description**: Clinical trial investigating whether FDA-approved drug glycerol phenylbutyrate, a fatty acid compound that facilitates protein folding, can improve retinal function in patients with ATF6-caused achromatopsia.

### Therapeutic Approaches

- **Class 1 mutations**: Strategies focusing on improving protein trafficking out of ER
- **Class 3 mutations**: Require gene therapy to introduce functional ATF6 bZIP transcriptional activator or gene editing to repair primary ATF6 nucleotide alterations

## Key Therapeutic Considerations

### Optimal Treatment Window

- Gene therapy appears most effective when administered at younger ages
- Suggested ideal age may be before approximately 7 years old
- After this point, visual cortex has mostly developed and primary structural loss of cone photoreceptors has occurred
- BCM mouse studies show efficacy consistently decreasing in older mice

### Current Challenges

1. **CNGA3 vs CNGB3 efficacy difference**: CNGB3 mutations result in no protein expression, while CNGA3 mutations produce defective proteins that may interfere with therapeutic protein function
2. **High-dose inflammation**: Severe ocular inflammation observed in pediatric patients at highest doses
3. **Limited therapeutic window**: Age-related decline in treatment efficacy
4. **Outcome measure development**: Need for sensitive and quantitative endpoints for clinical trials

## 2025-2026 Additions

### Gene Therapy Clinical Trial Updates

#### AGTC Achromatopsia Trials (2025)

12-month data for Phase 1/2 trials (45 patients total):
- **CNGB3**: 2 adults + 2 pediatric patients at second-highest dose showed improved
  retinal sensitivity (static perimetry); 2 adults showed light discomfort improvement
- **CNGA3**: No consistent biologic activity observed
- **Differential efficacy hypothesis**: CNGB3 patients have no protein expressed
  (clean slate for gene therapy), while CNGA3 patients have defective protein that
  may interfere with therapeutic protein function

**Source**: Foundation Fighting Blindness / AGTC announcements (2025)

#### MeiraGTx Achromatopsia Trials (2025)

- AAV8-hCARp.hCNGB3 (subretinal) -- Phase I/II complete
- Safe and generally well tolerated in adults and children
- No consistent efficacy at 24 weeks, though individual trends in color vision
  and photo-aversion observed

#### BCM Gene Therapy Progress (2025)

- **BGTF-027 (Blue Gen Therapeutics)**: Intravitreal gene therapy encoding functional
  L-opsin for BCM. Phase 1 trial protocol in development (announced July 2025).
  $4.7M CIRM grant to Children's Hospital Los Angeles.
- **ADVM-062 (Adverum)**: Intravitreal AAV.7m8 vector for sustained L-opsin
  expression (preclinical stage)
- **Treatment window research (2025)**: "Molecular mechanisms limiting the AAV gene
  therapy treatment window in mouse models of blue cone monochromacy."
  *Communications Biology*, 2025. DOI: 10.1038/s42003-025-09045-0.
  Findings inform optimal patient age for intervention.
