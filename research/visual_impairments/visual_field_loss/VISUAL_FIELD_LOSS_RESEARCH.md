# Visual Field Loss: Accessibility Research Report

**Research Date:** December 2024
**Focus:** Hemianopia, tunnel vision, scotomas, peripheral and central vision loss

---

## Overview

Visual field loss affects approximately 25% of people with low vision, impacting how users perceive and interact with digital interfaces. This document covers research on various types of visual field defects and their implications for accessible design.

## Types of Visual Field Loss

### Central Field Loss
- **Cause:** Macular degeneration, diabetic maculopathy
- **Impact:** Loss of detailed central vision, difficulty reading, face recognition
- **Prevalence:** ~75% of low vision rehabilitation cases

### Peripheral Field Loss (Tunnel Vision)
- **Cause:** Glaucoma, retinitis pigmentosa, choroideremia
- **Impact:** Loss of side vision, mobility challenges, reduced spatial awareness
- **Prevalence:** ~25% of low vision cases

### Hemianopia
- **Cause:** Stroke, brain injury, tumors
- **Types:**
  - Homonymous: Same side lost in both eyes (left or right)
  - Bitemporal: Outer peripheral lost in both eyes (central tunnel)

### Scotomas
- **Definition:** Blind spots or areas of reduced sensitivity
- **Cause:** Various retinal and neurological conditions
- **Impact:** Unpredictable gaps in visual perception

## Reading with Visual Field Loss

### Hemianopia Reading Challenges

> "With longer words, readers with homonymous hemianopia can only see part of the word, leading to guessing. Reading with right-sided homonymous hemianopia is described as 'reading into nothingness.'"

**Research Finding:**
- Right-sided loss impacts forward reading span
- Left-sided loss impacts word beginning recognition
- Both require compensatory eye movements

### Tunnel Vision Reading

- Users may need smaller text to see more words at once
- Conflicts with typical "larger is better" guidelines
- Magnification can actually reduce usability

## Assistive Technology Solutions

### Digital Spectacles (DSpecs)
- Virtual reality-based peripheral field expansion
- Image remapping to relocate targets within remaining field
- Improved obstacle avoidance and object recognition
- [PMC Research](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7002244/)

### Head-Mounted Displays
- Acesight: Electronic visual aid for tunnel vision
- Tested with 57 patients
- Improvements in visual field, search ability, mobility
- [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10792-024-02974-5)

### Prism Glasses
- Redirect peripheral images to healthier retina areas
- Expand functional field of view
- Non-digital solution for daily activities

## Interface Design Recommendations

### For Peripheral Vision Loss

1. **Compact Layouts:** Keep important content close together
2. **Clear Navigation:** Minimize need for broad scanning
3. **Consistent Placement:** Fixed locations for key elements
4. **Motion Cues:** Use subtle animations to draw attention

### For Central Vision Loss

1. **Peripheral Awareness:** Important info at edges
2. **Large Touch Targets:** Compensate for eccentric viewing
3. **High Contrast:** Essential for reduced acuity
4. **Text Alternatives:** Audio descriptions, haptic feedback

### For Hemianopia

1. **Avoid Edge Placement:** Content on affected side may be missed
2. **Redundant Positioning:** Duplicate critical info
3. **Clear Reading Lines:** Aid text tracking
4. **Customizable Layout:** User-adjustable interface positions

## Testing and Simulation

### VR-Based Simulation (2024)
- Gaze-contingent tunnel vision using eye tracking
- Three visual tasks: motion perception, visual search, navigation
- [Springer Research](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10055-024-00987-0)

### Physical Simulators
- VisualEyes glasses for hemianopia simulation
- Fork in the Road low vision simulator kits
- Educational tools for awareness training

### Movie-Viewing Pupil Perimetry
- Novel assessment using pupil responses during video watching
- Tests hemianopia and glaucoma conditions
- [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00417-024-06733-1)
