## 1. Sechrest Et Al. (2023)

Citation:
`Blue Cone Monochromacy and Gene Therapy.`

Local artifacts:

- `papers/downloads/blue_cone_monochromacy/Sechrest_2023_BCM_Gene_Therapy_Review.html`
- `papers/downloads/blue_cone_monochromacy/Sechrest_2023_BCM_Gene_Therapy_Review.txt`
- `papers/downloads/blue_cone_monochromacy/Sechrest_2023_BCM_Gene_Therapy_Review.pdf.trace.html`

Granular findings:

- The review describes BCM as an X-linked disorder caused by mutations in the
  `OPN1LW/OPN1MW` gene cluster.
- It estimates BCM prevalence at approximately 1 in 100,000 people worldwide.
- Typical clinical features include poor visual acuity, severely impaired color
  discrimination, myopia, photophobia, and infantile nystagmus.
- The paper emphasizes that BCM can look superficially similar to
  achromatopsia, but differs genetically and in inheritance pattern.
- The review discusses structural retinal changes, including foveal outer
  nuclear layer thinning and shortened cone outer segments.
- Its main therapeutic focus is AAV gene therapy, including animal models,
  proof-of-concept work, and clinical-outcome measurement planning.

Project distillation:

- BCM should not be treated as "just color blindness" in repo guidance.
- Accommodations for BCM should consider acuity, photophobia, and eye-movement
  instability alongside color discrimination.
- Claims around future gene-therapy progress should stay grounded in review
  language unless tied to a specific newer trial source.
