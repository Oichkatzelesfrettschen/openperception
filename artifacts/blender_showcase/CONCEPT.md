# Blender Showcase Concept

This showcase is not a brand poster. It is a living concept model for the repo.

## What It Represents

The scene depicts one operational flow:

1. Research
2. Validation
3. Accommodations

That flow is meant literally:

- the left lane stands for primary sources, provenance, and synthesis notes
- the center lane stands for machine checks and evidence gates
- the right lane stands for transformed outputs that keep the same source usable
  under different perceptual needs

## What The Right Lane Must Show

The accommodations lane is only valid if it demonstrates transformation in
action, not accommodation as a label. The current concept requires three
distinct output modes:

- contrast-led
- guided-symbol-led
- depth-safe

Those are not marketing categories. They are concrete ways one source view can
be made more legible while preserving meaning.

## Depth Rule

The front plaque stays grounded in the stereoblindness synthesis:

- static cues first
- stereo optional
- motion supplemental

The current rendered plaque text is:

- `same source, adapted views`

That text is acceptable because it describes the transformation directly
without promising inspiration or lifestyle.

## Why This Is A Living Concept

The spec at `artifacts/blender_showcase/openperception_palette_showcase_spec.json`
is generated from:

- token files under `tokens/`
- the harmonized depth guide
- generated repo stats under `docs/generated/repo_stats.json`

That means the artifact can evolve with the repo's actual corpus and validation
surfaces instead of freezing as a one-off composition.

## Failure Modes To Avoid

This lane should reject:

- slogans
- buzzwords
- abstract virtue labels
- unreadable microtext
- decorative symbolism that does not map to repo behavior

If the scene stops reading as research passing through checks into adapted
outputs, it has drifted away from the repo.
